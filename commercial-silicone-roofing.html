What Is a Silicone Roof Coating?

A silicone roof coating is a liquid-applied roofing material that cures into a seamless, flexible, waterproof membrane over an existing commercial roof. Instead of removing the entire roof assembly, a qualified contractor inspects the roof, repairs weak areas, cleans the surface, reinforces seams and flashing details, and applies silicone coating at the proper thickness.

For many Staten Island commercial buildings, silicone coating is an excellent option when the existing roof is aging but still structurally sound. It can be used on many flat and low-slope roofing systems, including TPO, EPDM, modified bitumen, built-up roofing, metal roofing, and certain previously coated roof systems after adhesion testing.

Because silicone is highly resistant to UV exposure and ponding water, it is frequently chosen for commercial flat roofs where sun, snow, rain, freeze-thaw movement, and drainage challenges are constant concerns.

Why Staten Island Businesses Choose Silicone Roof Coatings

Leak Protection

Silicone forms a monolithic layer with no traditional seams, reducing common leak points around laps, flashings, drains, vents, HVAC curbs, and penetrations.

Less Disruption

Roof restoration is often faster and less disruptive than full replacement. Many businesses remain open while work is completed.

Reflective Surface

White reflective coating helps reduce heat absorption on commercial flat roofs during hot Staten Island summers.

Extends Roof Life

When the roof is a good candidate, silicone restoration can add years of service life and delay a costly tear-off.

Ponding Water Resistance

Silicone is widely used where occasional ponding water is present, though drainage problems should still be inspected and corrected when possible.

Lower Waste

Coating over an existing roof can reduce landfill waste compared with removing the entire old roof system.

Our Fluid-Applied Flat Roof Restoration Process

1. Commercial Roof Inspection

We inspect the roof membrane, seams, drainage, flashing, parapet walls, penetrations, prior patches, wet insulation concerns, and substrate condition.

2. Cleaning and Preparation

The roof is cleaned so the coating can properly bond. Dirt, debris, grease, loose coating, and contaminants are addressed before application.

3. Repairs and Reinforcement

Open seams, splits, punctures, blisters, flashing issues, drain areas, and roof penetrations are repaired. Reinforcement fabric may be embedded in high-stress areas.

4. Adhesion Testing and Primer

Some roofs require adhesion testing and primer to confirm compatibility between the existing roof and silicone coating system.

5. Silicone Coating Application

Silicone is applied by brush, roller, or spray equipment at the manufacturer-specified thickness to create a seamless protective membrane.

6. Final Inspection

We review coverage, details, drains, edges, penetrations, and workmanship so the finished roof coating is ready to protect your building.

Commercial Silicone Roof Coating FAQs

What is a silicone roof coating?

A silicone roof coating is a fluid-applied roofing membrane that cures into a seamless, waterproof layer over an existing commercial flat roof.

What is fluid-applied flat roof restoration?

Fluid-applied flat roof restoration means repairing and preparing the existing roof, then applying liquid roofing materials that cure into a continuous protective membrane.

Is silicone roof coating good for Staten Island commercial roofs?

Yes. Silicone roof coating is a strong option for many Staten Island commercial flat roofs because it resists UV exposure, rain, snow, freeze-thaw movement, and occasional ponding water.

Can silicone roof coating stop roof leaks?

Yes, when the roof is properly inspected, cleaned, repaired, reinforced, primed when needed, and coated at the correct thickness, silicone can seal many flat roof leaks.

Is silicone roof coating cheaper than roof replacement?

In many cases, silicone roof restoration is less disruptive and more cost-effective than a full tear-off, but the roof must be inspected to confirm it is a good candidate.

How long does a silicone roof coating last?

The service life depends on roof condition, coating thickness, preparation, drainage, weather, and maintenance. Many commercial silicone systems are designed to extend roof life for years when installed correctly.

Can silicone coating be applied over TPO roofing?

Yes, many TPO roofs can be coated after cleaning, repairs, adhesion testing, and primer when required by the coating manufacturer.

Can silicone coating be applied over EPDM roofing?

Yes, EPDM roofs can often be restored with silicone after proper cleaning, preparation, repairs, and adhesion testing.

Can silicone coating be applied over modified bitumen?

Yes. Modified bitumen roofs are common candidates for silicone restoration when the surface is stable and not saturated with moisture.

Can silicone coating be applied over metal roofing?

Yes. Silicone can be used on metal roofs to seal seams, fasteners, laps, and areas vulnerable to corrosion after proper prep and primer.

Does silicone roof coating help with energy savings?

White reflective silicone can reduce heat absorption on a commercial roof, which may help reduce cooling demand during warm weather.

Does silicone roof coating handle ponding water?

Silicone is one of the better coating choices for roofs with occasional ponding water, but drainage issues should still be inspected and corrected whenever possible.

What buildings are good candidates for silicone coating?

Warehouses, apartment buildings, offices, retail centers, restaurants, schools, churches, industrial buildings, and medical facilities can all be candidates if the existing roof is restorable.

Do you coat warehouse roofs?

Yes. Maximum Roofing provides warehouse roof coating and flat roof restoration throughout Staten Island.

Do you coat apartment building roofs?

Yes. We restore flat roofs on apartment buildings, mixed-use properties, and multifamily buildings.

Do you coat restaurant roofs?

Yes. We inspect kitchen exhaust areas, grease exposure, rooftop units, vents, seams, and flashing before coating restaurant roofs.

Do you coat office building roofs?

Yes. Silicone coating can be a strong restoration option for aging flat roofs on office buildings.

Can my business stay open during roof coating?

Usually yes. Silicone roof restoration is often less disruptive than full roof replacement, allowing many businesses to remain open.

How do you prepare a roof for silicone coating?

Preparation includes inspection, moisture checks, cleaning, repairs, seam reinforcement, flashing repair, adhesion testing, primer if needed, and coating application.

Do you power wash before coating?

Most roofs require cleaning before coating so the silicone can bond properly. The exact cleaning method depends on roof type and condition.

What are signs my commercial roof needs restoration?

Common signs include recurring leaks, open seams, ponding water, aging membrane, cracks, punctures, deteriorated flashing, and high cooling bills.

When is roof replacement better than coating?

Replacement may be better when the roof has widespread wet insulation, structural damage, severe deterioration, major deck issues, or is beyond restoration.

Can silicone coating be recoated later?

Yes. Many silicone systems can be cleaned and recoated later, extending the roof system’s useful life.

Is silicone roof coating waterproof?

When installed properly, silicone creates a seamless water-resistant membrane designed to protect commercial flat roofs from leaks.

Will silicone fix bad drainage?

Silicone can resist ponding water, but it does not correct structural drainage problems. Drains, scuppers, gutters, and slope should be inspected.

How thick is silicone roof coating?

Thickness depends on the system, manufacturer requirements, roof condition, and warranty goal. Commercial systems are applied to specified wet and dry mil thicknesses.

How long does silicone coating installation take?

Many projects can be completed within a few days, depending on roof size, preparation, repairs, coating thickness, access, and weather.

Does silicone coating smell?

There may be some odor during application, but the process is generally less disruptive than full tear-off roof replacement.

Do you provide emergency roof leak repair before coating?

Yes. We can inspect and repair active leaks before recommending a full silicone restoration system.

How often should a coated roof be inspected?

Commercial roofs should usually be inspected at least once or twice per year and after major storms.

What maintenance does a silicone roof need?

Maintenance includes clearing debris, checking drains, inspecting seams and flashings, looking for punctures, and addressing small issues quickly.

Can silicone coating be walked on?

Limited foot traffic is usually acceptable after curing, but walk pads may be recommended around HVAC units and service areas.

Does silicone coating work in winter?

Coating work depends on temperature, weather, moisture, and manufacturer application requirements. We evaluate conditions before scheduling.

Is silicone better than acrylic roof coating?

Silicone generally performs better with ponding water and UV exposure, while acrylic may be used in other situations. The best option depends on the roof.

Is silicone better than aluminum roof coating?

Silicone usually provides a more seamless, flexible restoration system, while aluminum coatings are often used for reflectivity on certain asphalt-based roofs.

Can silicone coating go around HVAC units?

Yes. HVAC curbs, penetrations, supports, pipes, vents, and drains are critical detail areas that must be repaired and reinforced correctly.
