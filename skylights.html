Installation Process

How Skylights Are Installed

1. Roof Inspection

The roof structure, location, slope, and surrounding materials are evaluated.

2. Skylight Planning

The proper skylight size, placement, and installation method are determined.

3. Roof Preparation

The roof opening and surrounding area are prepared for installation.

4. Skylight Installation

The skylight, flashing, and waterproofing components are installed correctly.

5. Final Inspection

The skylight, flashing, seals, and surrounding roof area are inspected.

Skylight Repair

Common Skylight Problems We Repair

Leaking Skylights

Leaks can develop from damaged flashing, worn seals, installation issues, or water intrusion around the skylight.

Damaged Flashing

Flashing failures around skylights are a common cause of roof leaks.

Cracked Skylight Components

Damaged glass, seals, or skylight parts may require repair or replacement.

Condensation Problems

Moisture buildup can result from ventilation issues or skylight conditions.

Old Skylight Replacement

Older skylights may need replacement due to age, damage, or repeated leaks.

Storm Damage

Wind, hail, and falling debris can damage skylights and surrounding roofing.

Skylight Maintenance

Protecting Your Skylight System

  • Inspect skylight flashing regularly.
  • Check for leaks after storms.
  • Keep roof drainage areas clear.
  • Inspect seals and surrounding materials.
  • Address moisture problems quickly.
  • Maintain proper attic ventilation.
  • Schedule professional inspections.
Repair vs Replacement

Should You Repair or Replace a Skylight?

Small issues such as flashing problems, damaged seals, or minor leaks can often be repaired. Older skylights with repeated problems or major damage may require replacement.

Maximum Roofing evaluates the skylight unit, flashing, surrounding roof materials, drainage, and overall condition before recommending the best option.
