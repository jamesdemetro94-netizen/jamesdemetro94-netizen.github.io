Installation Process

How Cedar Roofing Is Installed

1. Roof Inspection

The roof structure, decking, ventilation, slope, drainage, and existing materials are evaluated.

2. Preparation

Damaged materials are removed and the roof deck is prepared for the new cedar system.

3. Underlayment & Flashing

Water protection layers and flashing details are installed before cedar materials are applied.

4. Cedar Installation

Cedar shakes or shingles are installed with proper spacing, fastening, and alignment.

5. Final Inspection

Completed roofing details, flashing, edges, and drainage areas are reviewed.

Cedar Roof Repair

Common Cedar Roofing Problems We Repair

Broken Cedar Shakes

Cracked or damaged shakes can allow water into the roofing system and should be replaced promptly.

Missing Cedar Shingles

Wind and storms can remove cedar pieces and leave exposed areas requiring repair.

Cedar Roof Leaks

Leaks may come from damaged cedar, flashing failures, valleys, chimneys, skylights, or moisture issues.

Storm Damage

Branches, wind, hail, and severe weather can damage cedar roofing systems.

Moss & Moisture Problems

Excess moisture and organic growth can affect cedar performance and appearance.

Flashing Repairs

Metal flashing around roof transitions must remain properly installed to prevent water intrusion.

Cedar Roof Maintenance

Protecting Your Cedar Roof

  • Schedule regular roof inspections.
  • Keep gutters and drainage systems clear.
  • Remove damaging debris from the roof.
  • Monitor moss, algae, and moisture conditions.
  • Inspect flashing around chimneys and skylights.
  • Maintain proper attic ventilation.
  • Repair damaged cedar pieces quickly.
Repair vs Replacement

Should You Repair or Replace a Cedar Roof?

Small areas of damage can often be repaired by replacing individual cedar shakes or shingles. When deterioration is widespread, replacement may be the better long-term solution.

Maximum Roofing evaluates the condition of the cedar, decking, flashing, ventilation, and overall roofing system before recommending the right approach.
