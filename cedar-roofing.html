Cedar Roofing Services

Professional Cedar Shake & Cedar Shingle Roofing

Cedar roofing requires specialized installation methods to protect the wood, manage moisture, and maintain the natural appearance homeowners choose cedar for. Cedar Shake Roofing

Cedar shakes provide a natural textured appearance and are commonly selected for traditional, historic, and high-end residential properties. Proper spacing, fastening, ventilation, and flashing are critical.

Cedar Shingle Roofing

Cedar shingles provide a more uniform appearance while offering the warmth and character of natural wood roofing.

Cedar Roof Repair

Damaged cedar pieces, leaks, flashing failures, storm damage, and moisture problems can often be addressed with professional repairs.

Cedar Roof Replacement

When a cedar roof reaches the end of its useful life, replacement involves proper removal, deck evaluation, underlayment, flashing, ventilation, and new cedar installation.

Cedar Flashing Repair

Chimneys, valleys, skylights, walls, dormers, and roof edges require properly installed flashing to prevent water intrusion.

Cedar Roof Maintenance

Regular inspections help identify damaged cedar, debris buildup, moss growth, ventilation concerns, and drainage issues.

Cedar Roofing Benefits

Why Homeowners Choose Cedar Roofing

Natural Appearance

Cedar provides a unique wood appearance that complements many architectural styles.

Energy Performance

Wood roofing has natural insulating characteristics and works together with proper attic ventilation.

Repairable System

Individual damaged cedar shakes or shingles can often be replaced without replacing the entire roof.

Installation Process

How Cedar Roofing Is Installed

1. Roof Inspection

The roof structure, decking, ventilation, slope, drainage, and existing materials are evaluated.

2. Preparation

Damaged materials are removed and the roof deck is prepared for the new cedar system.

3. Underlayment & Flashing

Water protection layers and flashing details are installed before cedar materials are applied.

4. Cedar Installation

Cedar shakes or shingles are installed with proper spacing, fastening, and alignment.

5. Final Inspection

Completed roofing details, flashing, edges, and drainage areas are reviewed.
