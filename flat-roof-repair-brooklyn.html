Roofing Systems We Service

Flat Roof Leak Repair

We trace water entry from seams, drains, flashing, parapet walls, vents, and roof edges so the repair addresses the source instead of only the ceiling stain.

Torch Down Roofing

Heavy-duty modified bitumen systems for low-slope roofs. We handle membrane patches, seam issues, flashing tie-ins, and leak repairs where heat-applied roofing is already installed.

TPO Roofing

Energy-efficient, white thermoplastic single-ply membranes. We specialize in hot-air welded seam repairs, flashing corrections, puncture patches, and commercial leak service.

EPDM Roofing

Durable rubber membrane roofing for low-slope buildings. We repair punctures, open laps, edge details, pipe boots, and drainage-related leak points.

Metal Roofing

For metal panels, transitions, fasteners, counter-flashing, and roof-edge details, we look for movement, rust, failed sealant, and water paths that cause recurring leaks.

Structural Waterproofing

Waterproofing support for roof edges, parapet walls, penetrations, masonry tie-ins, and vulnerable transitions where rainwater can enter the building envelope.

TPO, EPDM, Metal, Torch Down & Modified Bitumen Roofing in Brooklyn

Brooklyn buildings can use different roofing systems depending on the structure, slope, drainage, and use of the property. Maximum Roofing works with common flat and low-slope systems including TPO roofing, EPDM roofing, torch down roofing, modified bitumen roofing, and metal roofing.

Torch down and modified bitumen are common choices for flat and low-slope roofs. TPO is often used on commercial flat roofs because it is a single-ply roofing system. EPDM is a rubber roofing system used on many low-slope roofs. Metal roofing can appear on commercial buildings, additions, canopies, and sloped roof sections.

Maximum Roofing can inspect the existing roof system and explain whether a repair, patch, waterproofing detail, maintenance service, coating, or replacement is the better option for the building.
