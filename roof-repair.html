Common Roof Problems We Repair

Leaks and Water Intrusion

Water stains on ceilings, bubbling paint, damp insulation, and dripping water are signs that the roof should be inspected. Water can travel before it appears inside, so the visible stain is not always directly below the leak source.

Storm and Wind Damage

Strong winds can lift shingles, loosen flashing, damage ridge caps, move roof materials, or expose weak areas. Storm damage should be checked quickly before the next rainfall makes the problem worse.

Ponding Water on Flat Roofs

Flat roofs should drain properly. Standing water can stress seams, membranes, coatings, drains, and flashing details. We inspect drainage and repair vulnerable areas.

Flashing Failure

Flashing leaks often happen around chimneys, walls, skylights, vents, pipes, and roof edges. Proper flashing repair is important because caulk-only fixes usually do not last.

Damaged Roof Membranes

TPO, EPDM, torch down, and modified bitumen roofs can be damaged by foot traffic, weather, age, seams, punctures, rooftop equipment, and poor drainage.

Old Repairs That Failed

Some leaks come from previous patches that did not address the real problem. We look at the whole roof area to determine whether a better repair is needed.

Roof Repair FAQs

Do you provide roof repair in Staten Island?

Yes. Maximum Roofing provides roof repair throughout Staten Island for residential and commercial properties. We repair leaks, storm damage, missing shingles, flashing problems, skylight leaks, flat roof issues, TPO, EPDM, torch down, and other common roofing problems.

Do you offer emergency roof repair?

Yes. If your roof is actively leaking or has storm damage, emergency roof repair may be needed to help prevent additional interior damage. We inspect the roof, identify the source of the problem, and recommend the most practical repair.

How do I know if my roof needs repair?

Common signs include ceiling stains, missing shingles, lifted shingles, water dripping inside, bubbling paint, damaged flashing, sagging areas, ponding water, cracked roof materials, or recurring leaks after rain.

Can a leaking roof be repaired?

Many roof leaks can be repaired if the roof is still structurally sound and the damage is isolated. If the roof has widespread deterioration, wet insulation, or major structural issues, replacement may be a better option.

How quickly should a roof leak be repaired?

A roof leak should be inspected as soon as possible. Even a small leak can damage insulation, decking, drywall, electrical areas, and interior finishes if water continues entering the property.

Do you repair asphalt shingle roofs?

Yes. We repair asphalt shingle roofs, including missing shingles, cracked shingles, wind damage, ridge cap problems, nail pops, flashing leaks, and roof leaks caused by worn or damaged shingles.

Do you repair flat roofs?

Yes. Maximum Roofing repairs residential and commercial flat roofs, including TPO, EPDM, torch down, modified bitumen, coated roofs, and other low-slope roofing systems.

Do you repair TPO roofs?

Yes. TPO roof repairs may include seam repair, puncture patching, flashing repairs, heat-weld repairs, and leak repairs around rooftop equipment, vents, curbs, and drains.

Do you repair EPDM rubber roofs?

Yes. EPDM repairs may include patching punctures, fixing seams, correcting flashing problems, addressing adhesive failure, and repairing leaks on black rubber flat roofs.

Do you repair torch down roofing?

Yes. Torch down and modified bitumen repairs may include sealing cracks, repairing blisters, fixing open seams, repairing flashing, and addressing leaks around edges or penetrations.

Can roof flashing be repaired?

Yes. Flashing can often be repaired or replaced around chimneys, walls, skylights, vents, pipes, and roof edges. Flashing problems are one of the most common causes of roof leaks.

Do you repair chimney leaks?

Yes. Chimney leaks may come from damaged flashing, counter flashing, cracked masonry, failed sealant, or roof damage around the chimney. We inspect the area and recommend the right repair.

Do you repair skylight leaks?

Yes. Skylight leaks can come from failed flashing, old seals, cracked skylight components, improper installation, or surrounding roof damage. We inspect the skylight and roof area together.

Can you repair roof vents and pipe boots?

Yes. Roof vents and pipe boots can leak when rubber gaskets crack, flashing fails, sealant deteriorates, or surrounding shingles are damaged. These areas should be inspected during leak repair.

What causes flat roofs to leak?

Flat roofs can leak because of ponding water, open seams, punctures, aging membrane, clogged drains, flashing failure, rooftop equipment, blisters, cracks, or poorly completed previous repairs.

Can ponding water damage a flat roof?

Yes. Ponding water can stress membranes, seams, flashing, coatings, drains, and roof structure over time. Drainage should be inspected and repaired when possible.

Do you repair commercial roofs?

Yes. We repair commercial roofing systems on warehouses, offices, apartment buildings, retail properties, mixed-use buildings, and other commercial properties throughout Staten Island.

Do you repair residential roofs?

Yes. We repair residential roof leaks, shingle damage, flashing issues, skylight leaks, chimney leaks, storm damage, and other roofing problems on Staten Island homes.

Can storm damage be repaired?

Yes. Storm damage may include missing shingles, lifted roofing materials, damaged flashing, punctures, fallen branches, or leaks. We inspect the roof and determine whether repair or replacement is needed.

Can wind damage a roof without shingles falling off?

Yes. Wind can lift shingles, loosen fasteners, weaken seals, bend flashing, and create hidden openings even if shingles are still visible on the roof.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

Repair may be the best option when damage is isolated and the roof is otherwise in serviceable condition. Replacement may be better when damage is widespread, the roof is very old, or leaks are recurring.

How long does roof repair take?

Small repairs may be completed quickly, while larger repairs involving flat roof membranes, flashing, storm damage, or multiple leak areas may take longer depending on weather and roof condition.

How much does roof repair cost?

Roof repair cost depends on the roof type, repair size, materials, access, roof slope, damage severity, and whether the problem involves shingles, flashing, flat roofing, or membrane repairs.

Do you provide free estimates?

Can small roof leaks become big problems?

Yes. Small leaks can spread into insulation, decking, drywall, framing, and interior finishes. Repairing leaks early can help prevent more expensive damage.

Do you repair roof drains?

Yes. Roof drains, scuppers, gutters, and drainage areas should be inspected when flat roofs have ponding water or leaks near drainage points.

Can gutters cause roof leaks?

Yes. Clogged, damaged, or poorly draining gutters can cause water to back up, overflow, or damage fascia, roof edges, and nearby roofing materials.

Do you repair fascia and soffit damage?

Yes. Fascia and soffit issues may be connected to roof edge leaks, gutter problems, ventilation issues, or water damage. We can inspect these areas during roof repair.

Can roof repairs help prevent mold?

Roof repairs can help stop water intrusion that may contribute to moisture and mold problems. If water has already entered the building, interior drying and remediation may also be needed.

How often should my roof be inspected?

It is smart to inspect your roof at least once a year and after major storms, especially if your roof is older, flat, commercial, or has had previous leaks.

Do you repair modified bitumen roofs?

Yes. Modified bitumen roof repairs may include repairing cracks, seams, blisters, flashing, edges, and areas where water is entering the roofing system.

Can old roof patches be fixed?

Yes. Failed patches can often be removed or corrected. We inspect the old repair and surrounding roof area to determine why the patch failed.

Do you repair roof leaks around HVAC units?

Yes. Rooftop HVAC units can leak around curbs, flashing, supports, penetrations, and nearby seams. These details are common leak sources on commercial flat roofs.

Do you repair apartment building roofs?

Yes. Maximum Roofing repairs apartment building roofs, multifamily flat roofs, mixed-use roofs, and commercial roof systems throughout Staten Island.

Do you repair retail and warehouse roofs?

Yes. We repair commercial roofs on retail buildings, warehouses, offices, industrial buildings, and other commercial properties with flat or low-slope roofing systems.

What should I do during an active roof leak?

Move valuables away from the leak, place a container under dripping water if safe, avoid climbing on the roof during bad weather, and call a roofing contractor for inspection and repair.

Can roof repair be done in winter?

Some repairs can be completed in winter, depending on weather, temperature, materials, roof condition, and safety. Emergency leak repairs may still be needed during cold weather.

Do you repair roofs after heavy rain?

Yes. Heavy rain often reveals leaks around flashing, flat roof seams, drains, skylights, chimneys, valleys, and roof penetrations. We can inspect the roof after the weather clears.
