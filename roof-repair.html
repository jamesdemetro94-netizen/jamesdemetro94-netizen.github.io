Roof Problems We Repair

Roof Leaks

Our Roof Repair Process

1. Inspect the Problem

We review where the leak or damage appears and inspect the roof surface, flashing, seams, shingles, penetrations, drains, and nearby details.

2. Identify Likely Causes

Roof leaks can travel before showing inside. We look for the root issue instead of guessing from the interior stain alone.

3. Explain Repair Options

We explain whether the roof needs a simple repair, emergency temporary protection, larger repair, maintenance, restoration, or replacement planning.

4. Complete the Repair

Depending on the roof, repairs may include shingles, flashing, sealant, membrane patches, seam repairs, drain area work, or flat roof repairs.

5. Review Maintenance Needs

We explain what to watch for and how to reduce future issues through inspections, gutter care, flashing maintenance, and early leak response.

6. Plan Replacement if Needed

If the roof is beyond repair, we can recommend replacement options that fit the building and roof type.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

Repair may be enough for isolated damage. Replacement may be better when the roof has widespread failure, repeated leaks, old age, or serious decking problems.

How long does roof repair take?

Can you repair a roof in the rain?

Can you repair roofs in winter?

Some repairs can be performed in winter, but weather, temperature, safety, and product requirements affect what can be done.

How often should I inspect my roof?

Most roofs should be inspected at least once or twice per year and after major storms, high winds, or signs of leaks.

Can roof repair prevent replacement?

Timely repairs can extend roof life and delay replacement when the roof is otherwise in good condition.
