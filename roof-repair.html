Roof Repair vs. Roof Replacement

A roof should usually be repaired when the damage is localized and the rest of the roof is still performing. Replacement becomes more likely when the roof is very old, has widespread deterioration, has multiple leaks, or has structural damage.

ProblemRepair Usually Makes SenseReplacement May Make Sense
One small leakYesNo, unless roof is failing overall
Missing shinglesYesOnly if damage is widespread
Bad flashingYesSometimes if surrounding roof is deteriorated
Rotten deckingSometimesOften if large areas are rotten
Multiple recurring leaksSometimesOften

Roof Repair Insurance Claims

Insurance may cover roof repair when damage is sudden and accidental, such as wind, storm, hail, or tree impact. Normal wear and tear, age, neglect, and maintenance problems are often not covered. Maximum Roofing can inspect the roof, document visible damage, and explain what we find so you can speak with your insurance carrier.
