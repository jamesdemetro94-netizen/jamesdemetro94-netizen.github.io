Executive Summary

Roof Repair Starts With Finding the Real Cause

A roof leak may appear in one location while entering through another. Water can move under shingles, through insulation, across decking, along framing, through deck flutes, or inside wall cavities before it becomes visible indoors.

Maximum Roofing reviews the entire problem area rather than covering only the visible stain. Depending on the roof type, repair may involve shingles, underlayment, flashing, membranes, seams, laps, drains, skylights, chimneys, pipe boots, parapets, curbs, roof edges, coatings, or damaged decking.

When damage is isolated, repair may preserve the roof and extend its service life. When deterioration is widespread, moisture is trapped, or leaks keep returning, restoration or replacement planning may be the stronger long-term option.

Weather Damage

Storm, Wind, Branch and Debris Damage

Storm damage may include missing shingles, lifted seals, broken ridge caps, punctured membranes, damaged flashing, loose metal, displaced rooftop materials, clogged drainage, branch impact, and interior water intrusion.

Some damage is obvious, while other conditions are hidden. Wind can weaken shingle seals or membrane edges without removing materials completely. After major weather events, vulnerable roof areas should be inspected before the next storm.

We can document visible roof conditions, photos, and repair recommendations. Insurance coverage is controlled by the policy and carrier.

Project Planning

Roof Repair vs. Restoration vs. Replacement

OptionMay Be Appropriate WhenImportant Considerations
Localized repairDamage is isolated and the surrounding roof remains serviceable.The actual cause must be identified, and repair materials should be compatible.
RestorationThe roof is aging but structurally suitable, reasonably dry, and compatible with restoration.Repairs, cleaning, preparation, moisture evaluation, and coating compatibility are required.
Partial replacementOne roof area is failing while other sections remain serviceable.Transitions between old and new work require careful design.
Full replacementThere is widespread deterioration, repeated leakage, wet insulation, deck damage, or end-of-life materials.Replacement allows damaged components to be removed and the assembly to be rebuilt.
Warranty and Protection

Roof Repair Warranty Guide

Contractor Workmanship Warranty

A workmanship warranty addresses the repair work described in the written agreement. Scope and duration depend on the repair type, surrounding roof condition, access, materials, and project terms.

Manufacturer Material Coverage

Some repair products may carry limited manufacturer coverage. Material coverage does not automatically include labor, leaks from unrelated roof areas, drainage problems, abuse, or existing system failures.

Existing Roof Limitations

A localized repair cannot guarantee unrelated portions of an older or deteriorated roof. The proposal should clearly identify the repair area, observed limitations, and recommended follow-up work.

  • Keep written proposals and invoices
  • Document the repaired roof area
  • Report recurring leakage promptly
  • Avoid unauthorized alterations
  • Maintain drains and roof access areas
  • Schedule follow-up inspection when recommended

Learn more in our roofing warranty guide.

Preventive Care

How to Reduce Future Roof Leaks

Inspect After Storms

Wind, branches, debris, snow, and heavy rain can expose weak areas or create new damage.

Keep Drainage Clear

Clean gutters, drains, scuppers, and downspouts help water leave the roof.

Control Roof Traffic

Tools, service workers, equipment, and repeated walking can damage membranes and shingles.

Repair Small Defects Early

Loose flashing, punctures, missing shingles, and open seams can become larger problems.

Track Repair History

Photos and records help identify repeat leak locations and support long-term planning.

Schedule Regular Inspections

Periodic inspections help identify damage before interior leakage becomes severe.

Moisture and Decking

Roof Repair Frequently Asked Questions

Do you provide roof repair in Staten Island?

Yes. Maximum Roofing provides residential and commercial roof repair throughout Staten Island and additional New York City service areas.

Do you offer emergency roof leak repair?

Yes. We provide emergency leak inspection, temporary stabilization where appropriate, and permanent repair planning.

How quickly should a roof leak be inspected?

As soon as safely possible. Ongoing water can damage insulation, decking, drywall, electrical systems, framing, and interior finishes.

Can a leaking roof be repaired?

Many leaks can be repaired when damage is isolated and the surrounding roof remains serviceable. Widespread damage may require larger work.

Do you repair shingle roofs?

Yes. We repair missing, lifted, cracked, and wind-damaged shingles, ridge caps, flashing, valleys, and related leak areas.

Do you repair flat roofs?

Yes. We repair TPO, EPDM, torch down, modified bitumen, coated roofs, and other commercial and residential low-slope systems.

Do you repair TPO roofs?

Yes. TPO repairs may include compatible membrane patches, heat-welded seam repair, flashing, curbs, drains, and penetrations.

Do you repair EPDM roofs?

Yes. EPDM repairs may include punctures, seam tape, primer, flashing, terminations, and compatible membrane patches.

Do you repair torch down roofs?

Yes. Torch down and modified bitumen repairs may include splits, cracks, blisters, open laps, flashing, and punctures.

Do you repair skylight leaks?

Yes. Skylight leaks may involve flashing, seals, glazing, condensation, or surrounding roof damage.

Do you repair chimney leaks?

Yes. Chimney leaks may involve step flashing, counterflashing, masonry joints, sealants, cricket areas, or nearby roof damage.

Can wind damage a roof without removing shingles?

Yes. Wind can weaken seals, lift edges, loosen fasteners, bend flashing, and create hidden openings.

Can storm damage be repaired?

Often yes. The repair depends on the roof type, damage extent, material availability, moisture, and surrounding roof condition.

Should I repair or replace my roof?

Repair may be appropriate for isolated damage. Replacement may be stronger when deterioration, moisture, or repeated leaks are widespread.

Can repairs be completed in winter?

Some repairs can be completed in cold weather, but safety, moisture, temperature, and product requirements control what is possible.

Do roof repairs come with warranties?

Repair warranties depend on the written agreement, repair type, materials, surrounding roof condition, and project limitations.

What affects roof repair cost?

Cost depends on roof type, damage size, access, safety, materials, leak complexity, moisture, and deck condition.
