What Is Torch Down Roofing?

Torch down roofing is a modified bitumen roofing system commonly used on flat and low-slope roofs. The membrane is asphalt-based and reinforced for flexibility, durability, and weather resistance.

During installation, trained roofers use controlled heat to bond membrane layers to the roof surface. Proper preparation, safe application, seam work, flashing details, and drainage planning all affect long-term performance.

This type of roofing is commonly used on commercial buildings, apartment buildings, garages, extensions, porches, and roof areas where shingles are not appropriate because the slope is too low.

Why Property Owners Choose Modified Bitumen

Durable Flat Roof Protection

Modified bitumen systems are designed for low-slope roof conditions where water, sun, wind, and temperature changes affect the roof daily.

Repairable System

Many flat roof leaks can be repaired when caught early, especially around seams, penetrations, edges, flashing, and drainage areas.

Layered Roofing Strength

Depending on the system, torch down roofing can include base layers and cap sheets for added protection and performance.

Good for Low-Slope Roofs

It is often used where asphalt shingles are not a good fit because the roof pitch is too low.

Residential & Commercial

It can be used on many commercial buildings and residential roof areas like garages, porches, dormers, and additions.

Local Weather Resistance

A properly installed flat roof system helps protect against Staten Island rain, snow, summer heat, wind, and freeze-thaw cycles.

Our Flat Roof Repair & Installation Process

1. Roof Inspection

We inspect membrane condition, seams, flashing, drains, gutters, parapet walls, penetrations, ponding water, soft spots, prior patches, and visible roof deck issues.

2. Tear-Off or Surface Preparation

Depending on the roof condition, the project may require tear-off, substrate repairs, insulation, cover board, or preparation of a suitable existing roof surface.

3. Base Layer and Detail Work

We prepare details around drains, edges, walls, skylights, vents, HVAC curbs, pipes, and transitions because many flat roof leaks begin at these areas.

4. Membrane Installation

The modified bitumen membrane is installed according to the selected system. Proper overlaps, heat control, seam bonding, and safety procedures are critical.

5. Flashing and Penetrations

Flashing is installed or repaired around roof edges, parapet walls, penetrations, drains, and transitions to create a complete water-shedding system.

6. Final Walkthrough

We review seams, details, drainage areas, workmanship, and cleanup before considering the project complete.

Torch Down Roofing vs. Other Flat Roof Systems

Roofing System Best For Important Notes
Torch Down / Modified Bitumen Flat and low-slope roofs needing durable asphalt-based membrane protection. Requires experienced installers and proper safety procedures.
TPO Roofing Commercial white reflective single-ply membrane roofs. Often used for energy-conscious commercial flat roofs.
EPDM Roofing Rubber membrane roofs needing flexibility and weather resistance. Common on commercial and residential low-slope roofs.
Silicone Roof Coating Restoring an existing roof that is still a good coating candidate. Not a replacement for roofs with severe saturation or structural problems.

Common Flat Roof Problems We Repair

Open Seams

Separated seams are a common flat roof leak source and should be addressed before water spreads into the roof system.

Failed Flashing

Edges, walls, skylights, vents, drains, and HVAC curbs require careful flashing to prevent water entry.

Ponding Water

Standing water can stress the membrane, weaken seams, and accelerate deterioration if drainage is not improved.

Cracks and Splits

Age, movement, weather exposure, and poor drainage can lead to cracks, splits, and brittle areas.

Punctures

Foot traffic, tools, branches, rooftop equipment, and storms can puncture or damage flat roof membranes.

Old Patch Failures

Temporary repairs often fail over time. We inspect previous patches and recommend a proper repair plan.

Flat Roof & Torch Down Roofing FAQs

These answers cover common questions about modified bitumen roofing, flat roof leaks, maintenance, repair options, replacement timing, safety, cost factors, and service availability in Staten Island.

Basics

What is torch down roofing?

Torch down roofing is a modified bitumen flat roofing system installed with controlled heat to bond membrane layers to the roof surface.

What is a modified bitumen roof?

Modified bitumen is an asphalt-based roofing membrane reinforced with polymers for flexibility, durability, and weather resistance on flat and low-slope roofs.

Where is torch down roofing commonly used?

It is commonly used on commercial buildings, apartment buildings, garages, porches, dormers, additions, and other low-slope roof areas where shingles are not suitable.

Is torch down roofing good for flat roofs in Staten Island?

Yes. It can be a durable option for many Staten Island flat roofs when the roof is properly prepared, installed safely, flashed correctly, and maintained over time.

Is torch down roofing waterproof?

When installed correctly, torch down roofing creates a strong water-resistant membrane. Performance depends on seams, flashing, drainage, roof details, and workmanship.

Installation & Replacement

How is a torch down roof installed?

The roof is inspected and prepared first. Depending on the system, roofers may install a base layer, cap sheet, flashing, edge details, and membrane seams using controlled heat and approved methods.

Can torch down roofing be installed over an existing roof?

Sometimes, but only if the existing roof is dry, stable, code-compliant, and suitable for the selected system. Many roofs require removal before a new system is installed.

How long does torch down installation take?

Project length depends on roof size, access, tear-off needs, weather, repairs, insulation, drainage, and details such as drains, parapet walls, vents, skylights, and roof edges.

Can torch down roofing be installed in winter?

It depends on temperature, moisture, wind, safety, roof condition, and product requirements. A roof inspection helps determine whether installation or repair can be performed safely.

When should a torch down roof be replaced?

Replacement may be needed when there are chronic leaks, wet insulation, widespread membrane deterioration, damaged decking, poor drainage, or repeated failed repairs.

Repair & Leak Problems

Can torch down roofing be repaired?

Yes. Many leaks, punctures, open seams, flashing problems, cracks, and damaged areas can be repaired after the roof is inspected.

What causes torch down roof leaks?

Common causes include open seams, failed flashing, ponding water, punctures, cracked membrane, drain issues, aging materials, and poorly sealed roof penetrations.

What are signs my flat roof needs repair?

Signs include interior stains, active leaks, bubbles, cracks, open seams, loose flashing, soft spots, standing water, damaged patches, and visible membrane wear.

Can ponding water damage a torch down roof?

Yes. Standing water can stress seams, accelerate membrane deterioration, and lead to leaks if drainage issues are not corrected.

Do you provide emergency flat roof leak repair?

Yes. Maximum Roofing provides emergency flat roof leak inspection and repair for torch down and modified bitumen roofs in Staten Island.

Inspection & Maintenance

How do you inspect a torch down roof?

We check seams, laps, flashing, drains, penetrations, membrane condition, soft spots, ponding areas, cracks, prior patches, and roof edges.

How often should a flat roof be inspected?

A flat roof should generally be inspected once or twice per year and after major storms, especially if the building has a history of leaks or drainage problems.

Does torch down roofing need maintenance?

Yes. Maintenance may include clearing debris, checking drains, inspecting seams, reviewing flashing, repairing small issues early, and watching for ponding water.

Can a torch down roof be coated?

Some torch down roofs can be coated after cleaning, repairs, and adhesion testing. The roof must be a good candidate for the selected coating system.

What makes flat roof leaks hard to find?

Water can travel under the membrane or through insulation before appearing indoors. That is why seams, flashing, drains, penetrations, and roof slope all need to be checked carefully.

System Comparisons

Is torch down better than EPDM?

It depends on roof design, budget, drainage, building use, and roof condition. EPDM is a rubber membrane, while torch down is an asphalt-based modified bitumen system.

Is torch down better than TPO?

TPO is a reflective single-ply membrane, while torch down is a modified bitumen membrane. The better option depends on the building, roof condition, energy goals, and installation requirements.

Is torch down better than silicone coating?

Silicone coating is usually a restoration option for an existing roof, while torch down is a roof membrane system. An inspection determines which option makes sense.

What is the difference between one-ply and two-ply torch down?

Two-ply systems usually include a base sheet and cap sheet for added protection. Some smaller or specific applications may use a single cap sheet depending on system design.

What is a torch down cap sheet?

A cap sheet is the top layer of a modified bitumen roof system. It may have a granulated or smooth surface depending on the product and roof application.

Safety & Materials

Is torch down roofing safe?

Torch down installation involves open flame and should only be performed by trained roofing professionals using proper safety procedures and precautions.

What thickness is torch down roofing?

Membranes come in different specifications. The right system depends on roof type, slope, traffic, exposure, drainage, and performance requirements.

How long does a torch down roof last?

Lifespan depends on installation quality, materials, roof slope, drainage, maintenance, weather exposure, and whether small problems are repaired early.

What can shorten the life of a flat roof?

Poor drainage, standing water, bad flashing, open seams, heavy foot traffic, clogged drains, storm damage, trapped moisture, and delayed repairs can shorten roof life.

Can foot traffic damage a torch down roof?

Yes. Heavy or careless foot traffic can damage membrane surfaces, seams, and flashing. Walk pads may be recommended around rooftop equipment or frequent access areas.
