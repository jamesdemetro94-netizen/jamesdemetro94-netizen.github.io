What Is Torch Down Roofing?

Torch down roofing is a modified bitumen roofing system commonly used on flat and low-slope roofs. The membrane is asphalt-based and reinforced for better flexibility and performance. During installation, a trained roofer uses controlled heat to bond the membrane to the roof surface and create a strong protective layer.

Torch down roofing is often used on commercial buildings, apartment buildings, garages, extensions, porches, and low-slope roof areas where shingles are not appropriate. It is valued for durability, repairability, and layered waterproofing protection when installed correctly.

Because torch down work involves heat and specialized installation methods, it should only be handled by experienced roofing professionals. Proper preparation, safe application, seam work, flashing details, and drainage planning all affect long-term performance.

Our Torch Down Roofing Process

1. Flat Roof Inspection

We inspect membrane condition, seams, flashing, drains, gutters, parapet walls, penetrations, ponding water, soft spots, prior patches, and the roof deck when visible.

2. Tear-Off or Surface Preparation

Depending on the condition of the existing roof, the project may require tear-off, substrate repairs, insulation, cover board, or preparation of a suitable existing roof surface.

3. Base Layer and Details

We prepare details around drains, edges, walls, skylights, vents, HVAC curbs, pipes, and transitions because most flat roof leaks begin at these vulnerable points.

4. Torch Down Membrane Installation

The modified bitumen membrane is installed according to the selected system. Proper overlaps, heat control, seam bonding, and safety procedures are critical.

5. Flashing and Penetration Work

Flashing is installed or repaired around roof edges, parapet walls, penetrations, drains, and transitions to create a complete water-shedding system.

6. Final Walkthrough

We review seams, details, drainage areas, workmanship, and cleanup before considering the project complete.

Common Torch Down Roof Problems We Repair

Open Seams

Separated seams are a common flat roof leak source and should be addressed before water spreads into the roof system.

Failed Flashing

Edges, walls, skylights, vents, drains, and HVAC curbs require careful flashing to prevent water entry.

Ponding Water

Standing water can stress the membrane, weaken seams, and accelerate deterioration if drainage is not improved.

Cracks and Splits

Age, movement, weather exposure, and poor drainage can lead to cracks, splits, and brittle areas.

Punctures

Foot traffic, tools, branches, rooftop equipment, and storms can puncture or damage flat roof membranes.

Old Patch Failures

Temporary repairs often fail over time. We inspect previous patches and recommend a proper repair plan.

Torch Down Roofing FAQs

What is torch down roofing?

Torch down roofing is a modified bitumen flat roofing system installed with heat to bond roofing membrane layers to the roof surface.

Is torch down roofing good for Staten Island flat roofs?

Yes. Torch down roofing can be a durable option for low-slope and flat roofs in Staten Island when installed by experienced roofing professionals.

What is modified bitumen roofing?

Modified bitumen is an asphalt-based roofing membrane reinforced with polymers to improve flexibility, durability, and weather resistance.

How long does a torch down roof last?

Lifespan depends on materials, installation quality, roof slope, drainage, maintenance, and weather exposure. Proper inspections and repairs can help extend service life.

Can torch down roofing be repaired?

Yes. Many torch down roof leaks, punctures, open seams, flashing issues, and damaged areas can be repaired after inspection.

When should a torch down roof be replaced?

Replacement may be needed when the roof has widespread deterioration, chronic leaks, wet insulation, damaged decking, or repeated failed repairs.

Is torch down roofing waterproof?

When properly installed, torch down roofing creates a strong water-resistant membrane for flat and low-slope roofs.

Is torch down roofing used on commercial buildings?

Yes. Torch down roofing is commonly used for commercial buildings, apartment buildings, garages, additions, and low-slope roof areas.

Can torch down roofing be used on residential roofs?

Yes. It is often used on residential flat roofs, porch roofs, garages, dormers, and extensions with low slope.

What are the benefits of torch down roofing?

Benefits can include durability, weather resistance, layered protection, repairability, and good performance on many flat roof applications.

What causes torch down roof leaks?

Common causes include open seams, poor flashing, ponding water, punctures, cracked membrane, drain issues, and aging materials.

Can ponding water damage torch down roofing?

Yes. Standing water can accelerate roof deterioration, stress seams, and lead to leaks if drainage is not addressed.

How do you inspect a torch down roof?

We check seams, laps, flashing, drains, penetrations, membrane condition, soft spots, ponding areas, cracks, and prior repair patches.

Can torch down roofing be coated?

Some torch down roofs can be coated after cleaning, repairs, and adhesion testing, depending on the condition and coating system.

Is torch down better than EPDM?

It depends on roof design, budget, slope, drainage, building use, and preferences. EPDM is rubber membrane roofing, while torch down is asphalt-based modified bitumen.

Is torch down better than TPO?

TPO is a single-ply reflective membrane, while torch down is modified bitumen. The best system depends on the building, roof condition, and goals.

Is torch down better than silicone coating?

Silicone coating is usually a restoration system over an existing roof, while torch down is a roofing membrane system. A roof inspection determines the better option.

Can torch down roofing be installed over an existing roof?

Sometimes, but only if the existing roof is suitable, dry, stable, code-compliant, and approved for the selected system. Many roofs require removal first.

How long does torch down installation take?

Project length depends on roof size, tear-off requirements, repairs, weather, access, and details like drains and parapet walls.

Does torch down roofing need maintenance?

Yes. Flat roofs should be inspected regularly, cleaned of debris, and checked for seam, flashing, and drainage problems.

How often should a torch down roof be inspected?

A commercial or flat roof should generally be inspected at least once or twice per year and after major storms.

What thickness is torch down roofing?

Torch down membranes come in different specifications. The right system depends on the roof type, traffic, slope, and performance requirements.

What is the difference between one-ply and two-ply torch down?

Two-ply systems include a base layer and cap sheet for added protection, while some smaller applications use a single cap sheet depending on system design.

What is a torch down cap sheet?

A cap sheet is the top layer of modified bitumen membrane, often surfaced with granules or a smooth finish depending on the product.

What are signs my torch down roof needs repair?

Signs include leaks, bubbles, cracks, open seams, loose flashing, soft spots, standing water, interior stains, and deteriorated patches.

Can torch down roofing be installed in winter?

Installation depends on temperature, weather, safety, moisture, and product requirements. We evaluate site conditions before scheduling.

Is torch down roofing safe?

Torch down installation involves open flame and must be performed by trained professionals using proper safety procedures and precautions.
