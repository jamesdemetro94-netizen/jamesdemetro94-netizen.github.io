Torch‑Down Roofing (Modified Bitumen) for Staten Island Commercial Flat Roofs

A torch‑down roof is a multi‑layer modified bitumen membrane system that is heat‑welded on site using a propane torch, creating a seamless, monolithic waterproof barrier. For Staten Island’s commercial flat roofs, this method delivers superior waterproofing and wind resistance because the heat‑fused seams eliminate weak points that can fail under ponding water or high‑wind uplift. Maximum Roofing Staten Island installs torch‑down systems that meet the demanding requirements of local building codes and the region’s freeze‑thaw cycles.

Torch‑Down Installation Process – Technical Protocol

  1. Substrate Preparation – The existing roof deck must be clean, dry, and free of debris, loose material, or moisture. Any residual gravel, old adhesive, or standing water is removed. A dry, sound substrate is critical for proper adhesion and long‑term performance.
  2. Heat‑Welding Protocol – Each modified bitumen ply is unrolled and heated with a propane torch until the underside bitumen begins to “bleed” out at the seams. This visible bleeding indicates that the material has reached the correct temperature for a molecular bond. The seam is then rolled with a weighted roller to ensure full contact and a watertight seal.
  3. Layering (Base & Cap) – A minimum two‑ply system is installed: a base ply (often SBS‑modified) adhered to the substrate, followed by a cap ply (APP‑modified) that provides UV resistance and a finished surface. For high‑traffic or high‑wind areas, a third ply or a granulated cap sheet is added. Each layer is offset to stagger seams and eliminate direct pathways for water infiltration.

Frequently Asked Questions About Torch‑Down Roofing

Why is torch‑down roofing better than EPDM for my Staten Island building?

Torch‑down (modified bitumen) provides a multi‑layer, heat‑fused system that is inherently more resistant to punctures and wind uplift than a single‑ply EPDM membrane. The heat‑welded seams create a monolithic barrier that does not rely on adhesives or mechanical fasteners, which can degrade over time. For Staten Island’s freeze‑thaw cycles and occasional high‑wind events, torch‑down offers superior long‑term reliability.

What safety measures does Maximum Roofing take during torch‑down installation?

We implement a comprehensive hot‑work safety plan that includes a designated fire watch, fire extinguishers within 30 feet of the work area, fire‑resistant drop cloths, and a pre‑work inspection of the roof deck for combustible materials. All crew members hold current OSHA 10‑hour construction safety cards, and we maintain a written fire‑prevention plan that is reviewed daily.

How often should my flat roof be inspected in Staten Island’s climate?

We recommend a professional inspection at least twice per year: once in the spring after the freeze‑thaw cycle and once in the fall before winter. Additional inspections should be performed after any severe storm or heavy snowfall. Staten Island’s humid subtropical climate accelerates wear on flashings and seams, making regular inspections essential for early detection of damage.

Does Maximum Roofing handle commercial roofing permits in NYC?

Yes. As a Staten Island‑based commercial roofing contractor, we manage all permit applications with the NYC Department of Buildings, including the required Professional Engineer (PE) stamp for structural modifications. We ensure that every torch‑down installation complies with local safety regulations and the NYC Building Code.

What is the expected lifespan of a modified bitumen roof?

A properly installed torch‑down modified bitumen roof typically lasts 20–25 years. The lifespan depends on the number of plies, the quality of the cap sheet, and the regularity of maintenance. With biannual inspections and prompt repairs, many systems exceed 25 years of service.
