What is EDPM Roofing?

EDPM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) is a synthetic rubber roofing membrane that offers exceptional flexibility, durability, and weather resistance. It is a popular choice for flat and low‑slope roofs on commercial and residential buildings throughout Staten Island.

EDPM roofing membranes are made from a blend of ethylene, propylene, and diene monomers, which gives them outstanding resistance to UV rays, ozone, and extreme temperatures. The material is highly flexible, allowing it to expand and contract with temperature changes without cracking. EDPM is also resistant to tears, punctures, and impact, making it a long‑lasting roofing solution.

EDPM roofing is available in various thicknesses (typically 45, 60, or 80 mils) and colors, with black being the most common due to its heat‑absorbing properties. While black EDPM absorbs heat, it also helps melt snow and ice in winter, reducing the risk of ice dams. For energy‑efficient applications, white EDPM is also available.

EDPM Roofing vs. TPO vs. PVC – Which is Best for Staten Island?

Each roofing material has its strengths. EDPM offers the best flexibility and weather resistance, making it ideal for flat roofs exposed to extreme temperature swings. TPO offers better energy efficiency due to its reflective surface, while PVC is more chemical resistant. How We Install EDPM Roofs – Step by Step

At Maximum Roofing, we follow a meticulous process to ensure a watertight, long‑lasting EDPM roof. Here is our step‑by‑step installation process:

  1. Inspection and Preparation: We start with a thorough inspection of the existing roof deck. We check for any damage, rot, or uneven areas that need to be addressed before installation. We also remove any debris and ensure the surface is clean and dry.
  2. Insulation and Cover Board: We apply a layer of polyiso insulation to improve energy efficiency and provide a smooth surface for the membrane. A cover board is then installed over the insulation to protect it and provide a stable base for the EDPM membrane.
  3. Membrane Layout: The EDPM membrane is rolled out and allowed to relax for a period to reduce wrinkles. We cut the membrane to fit the roof dimensions, leaving extra material for flashings and edges.
  4. Adhesion or Ballasting: Depending on the roof design, we either fully adhere the membrane using a bonding adhesive, ballast it with gravel or pavers, or mechanically attach it using fasteners and plates. Each method has its advantages, and we recommend the best option for your specific roof.
  5. Flashings and Details: We pay special attention to flashings around roof penetrations, edges, and corners. We use EDPM flashing material and seam tape or liquid flashing to create a seamless, leak‑proof barrier.
  6. Flood Test: After installation, we perform a flood test to verify the integrity of the roof. We fill the roof with water and check for any leaks. If any issues are found, we address them immediately.
  7. Final Inspection and Warranty: We conduct a final inspection to ensure everything meets our quality standards. We then provide a workmanship warranty and manufacturer’s warranty to give you peace of mind.

The entire installation process typically takes 2–5 days, depending on the size and complexity of the roof. Our team works efficiently while maintaining the highest quality standards.

EDPM Roofing Lifespan – How Long Does an EDPM Roof Last?

EDPM roofing is known for its durability and long lifespan. When properly installed and maintained, an EDPM roof can last 20–30 years or more. The lifespan depends on several factors:

  • Quality of Installation: Proper installation is the most important factor. Correct adhesion or ballasting, proper flashings, and attention to detail ensure a long‑lasting roof. At Maximum Roofing, we follow manufacturer guidelines and best practices to maximize lifespan.
  • Membrane Thickness: Thicker membranes (60 or 80 mil) tend to last longer than thinner ones (45 mil). We recommend 60 mil for most residential and commercial applications in Staten Island.
  • Climate and Weather: Staten Island’s climate includes hot summers, cold winters, and occasional storms. EDPM’s flexibility and UV resistance help it withstand these conditions. Regular maintenance can extend the roof’s life.
  • Maintenance: Regular inspections and maintenance, such as cleaning debris, checking flashings, and repairing minor damage promptly, can significantly extend the lifespan of an EDPM roof. We offer maintenance plans to keep your roof in top condition.
  • Manufacturer Warranty: Most EDPM manufacturers offer warranties ranging from 15 to 30 years. The warranty covers material defects, and some also cover installation workmanship. We provide a workmanship warranty on all our installations.
