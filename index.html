Roofing Information for Staten Island Property Owners

When Should You Repair or Replace a Roof?

Minor single-source problems can easily be fixed with target repairs. However, when composite asphalt or underlying substrates show systemic structural failure, wide-scale aging, or deck rotting, a safe replacement is necessary.

What Causes Roof Leaks?

The most common entry vectors are cracking edge flashings, compromised perimeter sealants, age-based physical shrinkage, clogged roof drainage paths, or intense weather impact lifting shingles clean off the deck.

How Long Does a Roof Last?

A typical residential shingle setup ranges between 20 to 25 years under normal conditions, while advanced single-ply commercial configurations like TPO or EPDM reliably push past 30 years with regular, professional upkeep.
