Common Commercial Roof Problems We Solve

Roof Leaks

Commercial leaks often begin around seams, flashing, drains, penetrations, old patches, rooftop units, and damaged membrane areas.

Ponding Water

Standing water on a flat roof can stress membranes, accelerate deterioration, and point to drainage problems that need attention.

Open Seams

Membrane seams are critical on flat roofs. When they separate or fail, water can enter below the roof system.

Flashing Failure

Parapet walls, curbs, vents, skylights, pipes, edges, and transitions all require proper flashing to prevent leaks.

Storm Damage

High winds, branches, debris, heavy rain, snow, and freeze-thaw cycles can damage commercial roof systems.

Aging Roof Systems

Older roofs can develop brittleness, cracks, shrinkage, punctures, membrane wear, and recurring leak issues.

Commercial Roof Maintenance Guide for Property Owners

A commercial roof should not be ignored until it leaks. Preventive maintenance helps protect the building, reduce emergency repairs, and give owners better control over roofing expenses.

Check Drains and Gutters

Blocked drains, scuppers, gutters, and downspouts can cause water to back up and increase roof stress.

Inspect Seams and Flashing

Open seams and flashing problems are common leak sources and should be repaired quickly.

Document Roof Condition

Photos, inspection notes, and repair records help owners plan maintenance and replacement more effectively.

Commercial Roofing Cost Factors

Commercial roofing cost depends on the roof, not just the square footage. Roof size matters, but so do access, tear-off, insulation, drainage, existing damage, roofing system, flashing details, rooftop equipment, safety requirements, and warranty goals.

Existing Roof Condition

Roofing System

TPO, EPDM, torch down, modified bitumen, and coatings all have different material and installation requirements.

How do I know if my commercial roof needs repair?

Signs include leaks, ceiling stains, ponding water, open seams, cracked membrane, deteriorated flashing, soft spots, punctures, and recurring maintenance issues.

How do I know if my commercial roof needs replacement?

Replacement may be needed when there is widespread deterioration, wet insulation, structural issues, repeated leaks, severe storm damage, or when repair costs no longer make sense.

Can my commercial roof be restored instead of replaced?

Sometimes. If the roof is structurally sound and not saturated with moisture, restoration with coating or targeted repairs may be possible.

How often should a commercial roof be inspected?

Commercial roofs should generally be inspected at least once or twice a year and after major storms, high winds, heavy snow, or major rooftop work.

What causes commercial roof leaks?

Common causes include failed flashing, open seams, clogged drains, punctures, ponding water, roof penetrations, poor installation, storm damage, and aging materials.

What is ponding water?

Ponding water is standing water that remains on a flat roof after rainfall. It can stress the roof system and should be evaluated.

What is commercial roof maintenance?

Commercial roof maintenance includes inspections, debris removal, drain checks, seam repairs, flashing review, puncture repairs, and documentation of roof condition.

Why is commercial roof maintenance important?

Maintenance helps catch small issues before they become major leaks, extends roof life, protects the building interior, and helps property owners plan ahead.

What is the difference between commercial roof repair and replacement?

Repair addresses specific issues, while replacement removes and replaces larger sections or the entire roof system when the existing roof is no longer reliable.

What is the difference between TPO and EPDM?

TPO is typically a white reflective single-ply membrane, while EPDM is a rubber roofing membrane commonly available in black and white options.

What is modified bitumen roofing?

Modified bitumen is an asphalt-based flat roofing membrane reinforced with polymers for flexibility and durability.

Is silicone roof coating a roof replacement?

No. Silicone coating is a restoration system applied over an existing qualified roof. It is not the same as replacing the roof assembly.
