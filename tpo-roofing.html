Understanding TPO Roofing

How Does a TPO Roofing System Work?

A TPO roofing system protects a building by creating a continuous waterproof membrane across the roof surface. Unlike traditional roofing materials that rely on multiple layers of asphalt, TPO uses a single-ply membrane that is manufactured in large rolls and installed over insulation or a cover board.

The seams between each sheet are heat welded together using specialized hot-air welding equipment. Properly completed welds create a continuous waterproof system designed to resist rain, snow, UV exposure, and normal weather conditions. Seam quality is one of the most important factors in the long-term performance of a TPO roof.

Every roofing system also includes flashing around walls, HVAC equipment, drains, skylights, vent pipes, and roof edges. These details help keep water from entering the building at transition points that are more vulnerable than the open field of the roof.

When properly designed, installed, inspected, and maintained, a TPO roofing system can provide durable protection for many commercial and residential low-slope buildings. The actual service life depends on factors such as membrane thickness, installation quality, climate, roof traffic, maintenance, drainage, and the overall condition of the roofing assembly.

What Is TPO Roofing?

TPO stands for thermoplastic polyolefin. It is a single-ply roofing membrane used on many commercial and low-slope roofs. TPO is popular because of its reflective surface, flexible membrane design, and heat-welded seams.

A complete TPO roofing system may include insulation, cover board, membrane, fasteners or adhesive, flashing, edge metal, drains, and rooftop penetration details.

TPO Roof Replacement

A TPO roof replacement may be recommended when a roof has widespread leaks, aging membrane, repeated repairs, damaged insulation, poor drainage, or extensive seam problems.

A replacement project allows the roofing system to be rebuilt with proper materials, flashing details, drainage improvements, and a new membrane system.
