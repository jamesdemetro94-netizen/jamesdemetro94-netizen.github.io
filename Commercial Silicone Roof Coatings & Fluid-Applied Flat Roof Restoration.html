Why Choose Silicone Roof Coatings for Your Staten Island Commercial Property?

Silicone roof coatings are the premier choice for flat and low‑slope commercial roofs in Staten Island’s humid subtropical climate. Unlike asphalt or modified bitumen, silicone forms a seamless, rubber‑like membrane that bonds directly to the existing roof surface. This fluid‑applied system cures into a monolithic layer that resists ponding water, UV degradation, and thermal shock.

  • Seamless & Leak‑Proof: No seams, no laps, no weak points. The coating is applied as a liquid and cures into a single, continuous membrane that stops leaks at their source.
  • Energy‑Efficient: White silicone coatings reflect up to 85% of solar radiation, reducing cooling costs by 15–30% during Staten Island’s hot summers.
  • Long‑Lasting: Properly applied silicone coatings can extend the life of your existing roof by 15–20 years, often with a 20‑year manufacturer warranty.
  • Low Maintenance: Silicone is inherently resistant to dirt, algae, and mildew. A simple annual inspection is usually all that’s needed.
  • Environmentally Friendly: Silicone coatings are low‑VOC and can be applied over many existing roof types, reducing landfill waste.

Our Fluid‑Applied Flat Roof Restoration Process

Every Staten Island commercial roof is different. Our process is tailored to your building’s specific needs, but the core steps remain the same:

  1. Thorough Inspection & Surface Preparation: We inspect the existing roof for damage, ponding water, and adhesion issues. We power wash, repair any blisters or splits, and ensure the surface is clean and dry.
  2. Primer Application (if needed): For certain substrates, we apply a silicone‑compatible primer to maximize adhesion.
  3. Fluid‑Applied Silicone Base Coat: Using rollers, brushes, or spray equipment, we apply the first coat of silicone at the manufacturer’s recommended thickness. This coat bonds to the substrate and creates the foundation of the monolithic membrane.
  4. Reinforcement Fabric (optional): In high‑stress areas (flashings, corners, drains), we embed a polyester reinforcement fabric into the wet silicone for added strength.
  5. Top Coat Application: We apply a second coat of silicone to achieve the final thickness (typically 20–30 mils dry). The coating cures within 24–48 hours, forming a seamless, watertight membrane.
  6. Final Inspection & Warranty: We perform a flood test or infrared scan to verify integrity. You receive a workmanship warranty and the manufacturer’s material warranty.

The entire process usually takes 2–5 days, depending on roof size and weather. Your business can remain open during the work.
