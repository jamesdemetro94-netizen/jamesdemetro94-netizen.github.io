Understanding Roofing Warranties

A roofing warranty explains what type of protection may be available after roofing work is completed. Warranty coverage can depend on the type of roof, materials installed, the scope of work performed, and the written warranty terms.

Roof warranties are generally divided into two main categories: contractor workmanship warranties and manufacturer material warranties.

Contractor Workmanship Warranty

A workmanship warranty covers the quality of the roofing installation or repair work performed by the contractor. It is designed to address issues related to installation methods, repair details, sealing, flashing, and other completed work.

Roof Repair Workmanship Coverage

Roof repairs may include workmanship protection for completed repair areas such as leak repairs, flashing repairs, shingle replacements, membrane repairs, and roof penetration sealing.

Roof Replacement Workmanship Coverage

Complete roof replacement projects may include longer workmanship protection because the contractor is responsible for installing the entire roofing system.

Roof Repair Warranty Protection

Roof repair warranties are different from full replacement warranties. A repair warranty generally applies to the specific area repaired and the materials used during that repair.

  • Roof leak repairs
  • Flashing repairs
  • Shingle repairs
  • Flat roof membrane repairs
  • TPO and EPDM repair areas
  • Roof penetration sealing

Manufacturer Material Warranties

Roofing manufacturers may provide warranties that protect against certain material defects. These warranties are separate from contractor workmanship coverage.

  • Asphalt shingle warranties
  • TPO membrane warranties
  • EPDM rubber roofing warranties
  • Modified bitumen warranties
  • PVC roofing warranties
  • Metal roofing warranties

TPO Roofing Warranty Protection

TPO roofing systems are commonly used for flat and low-slope roofs because of their durability and energy-efficient properties. Warranty options depend on the roofing system, materials selected, installation requirements, and warranty agreement.

TPO Thickness Typical Protection Common Use
45 Mil TPO Standard material protection options Projects where basic membrane protection is required
60 Mil TPO Enhanced durability and longer-term protection options Common choice for many commercial roofing applications
80 Mil TPO Premium membrane protection options Projects needing additional durability and weather resistance

EPDM Roofing Warranty Protection

EPDM rubber roofing systems are designed for long-term flat roof performance. Warranty coverage depends on the membrane system, installation details, maintenance, and the specific warranty agreement.

EPDM warranties may relate to material performance, while workmanship coverage depends on the contractor and completed work.

Modified Bitumen and Torch Down Roofing Warranties

Modified bitumen roofing systems, including torch-applied systems, are common flat roofing solutions. Warranty protection depends on the materials used, installation quality, and project requirements.

  • Membrane installation details
  • Seam performance
  • Flashing details
  • Roof penetration protection
  • Maintenance requirements

Asphalt Shingle Roofing Warranties

Asphalt shingle warranties are generally provided by the manufacturer and may cover eligible material defects. Contractor workmanship coverage is separate and relates to installation quality.

  • Shingle material protection
  • Installation workmanship
  • Roof ventilation requirements
  • Proper flashing installation

Commercial Roofing Warranty Protection

Commercial roofing warranties are often based on the complete roofing system, including membrane materials, insulation, flashing, drainage details, and installation requirements.

Commercial property owners should understand exactly what portions of the roof system are covered and what maintenance requirements apply.

What Roofing Warranties Usually Cover

  • Covered workmanship issues
  • Installation-related problems
  • Eligible material defects
  • Specified roofing system components
  • Covered repair requirements listed in the agreement

What Roofing Warranties Usually Do Not Cover

  • Normal aging and weathering
  • Damage from severe weather unless included
  • Improper maintenance
  • Unauthorized roof modifications
  • Damage caused by outside contractors
  • Existing roof problems not included in the agreement

Roof Maintenance and Warranty Protection

Regular roof inspections and proper maintenance help preserve roofing performance. Many warranty agreements require proper care and maintenance to keep coverage valid.

  • Schedule regular roof inspections
  • Keep drainage systems clear
  • Address leaks quickly
  • Document roof repairs
  • Follow manufacturer requirements
