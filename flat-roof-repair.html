Reliable Flat Roof Repair for Homes and Commercial Buildings

Flat roofs need a different repair approach than steep-slope roofs. Water does not shed as quickly, seams and flashings work harder, drains matter more, and small damage can lead to major leaks if it is ignored.

Maximum Roofing inspects flat roofs carefully to identify likely leak sources and determine whether your roof needs a targeted repair, a maintenance plan, a restoration system, or replacement. We focus on practical recommendations based on the condition of the roof, not one-size-fits-all answers.

Whether your building is in Great Kills, Port Richmond, St. George, New Dorp, Tottenville, Eltingville, Annadale, Westerleigh, Huguenot, or anywhere else in Staten Island, we can inspect your flat roof and explain the best next step.

Flat Roof Problems We Repair

Active Roof Leaks

We inspect interior water stains, rooftop conditions, seams, drains, and penetrations to locate likely sources of leaks.

Ponding Water

Standing water can stress flat roofing systems and may point to clogged drains, poor slope, low areas, or drainage design issues.

Open Seams

Flat roof seams can separate from age, movement, installation problems, water exposure, or storm damage.

Failed Flashing

Parapet walls, roof edges, vents, skylights, drains, pipes, chimneys, and HVAC curbs all require reliable flashing.

Punctures and Tears

Foot traffic, tools, branches, rooftop equipment, and debris can puncture or tear flat roof membranes.

Blisters and Cracks

Age, trapped moisture, sun exposure, freeze-thaw movement, and previous repairs can cause bubbles, cracks, and splits.

Our Flat Roof Repair Process

1. Interior and Exterior Review

We review where water appears inside and compare that with rooftop conditions because flat roof leaks can travel before showing up indoors.

2. Roof Surface Inspection

We check seams, drains, flashing, penetrations, rooftop equipment, membrane condition, ponding areas, punctures, and prior repairs.

3. Repair Recommendation

We explain whether the roof needs a small repair, larger repair, restoration, maintenance, or replacement planning.

4. Repair Work

Depending on the roof type, repairs may include seam work, membrane patches, flashing repair, drain area repair, coating touch-up, or modified bitumen repair.

5. Maintenance Guidance

After the repair, we explain how to reduce future leaks through cleaning, inspections, drain care, and early repair of small problems.

6. Replacement Planning if Needed

If the roof is beyond repair, we can recommend a replacement or restoration path that fits the building and roof condition.

Flat Roof Repair vs. Restoration vs. Replacement

OptionBest WhenWhat to Know
Flat roof repairThe issue is isolated, such as one leak, one flashing problem, or limited membrane damage.Often the fastest and most practical option when the roof still has useful life.
Flat roof restorationThe roof is aging but still structurally sound and not saturated with moisture.Silicone coating or restoration may extend roof life with less disruption than replacement.

Flat roof repair cost depends on the roof and the source of the problem. A simple puncture repair is very different from a roof with wet insulation, clogged drains, multiple old patches, and failing flashing. We inspect the roof before recommending the right scope of work.

A visible puncture may be simpler than a leak caused by hidden drainage, flashing, or membrane problems.

Roof Condition

How do I know where my flat roof is leaking?

Flat roof leaks can travel before appearing inside. We inspect seams, drains, flashing, roof penetrations, membrane damage, soft spots, and ponding areas to find likely leak sources.

What causes flat roof leaks?

Common causes include open seams, clogged drains, ponding water, cracked membrane, punctures, failed flashing, storm damage, old patches, and poor installation.

What is ponding water on a flat roof?

Ponding water is standing water that remains on the roof after rainfall. It can stress the roof membrane, seams, insulation, and drainage system.

Can ponding water be repaired?

Sometimes. Repairs may include clearing drains, improving drainage, adding crickets or tapered insulation during larger work, repairing low areas, or choosing a coating system that handles occasional ponding.

Can you repair TPO flat roofs?

Yes. We repair TPO membrane roofs, including seams, punctures, flashing, roof penetrations, and leak-prone areas.

Can you repair EPDM flat roofs?

Yes. We repair EPDM rubber roofs, including membrane punctures, seam problems, flashing details, and aging rubber roof areas.

Can you repair torch down flat roofs?

Yes. We repair torch down and modified bitumen flat roofs, including cracks, splits, open seams, flashing problems, and leak areas.

Can you repair silicone-coated flat roofs?

Yes. We inspect and repair silicone-coated roofs, including punctures, worn areas, seams, drainage issues, and coating damage.

When should a flat roof be repaired instead of replaced?

Repair may be the right choice when the roof has isolated issues and the overall roof system is still performing.

When should a flat roof be replaced?

Replacement may be needed when the roof has widespread deterioration, wet insulation, structural issues, repeated leaks, or repair costs no longer make sense.

Can a flat roof be restored instead of replaced?

Sometimes. If the roof is structurally sound and not saturated, restoration with coating or targeted repairs may extend roof life.

How long does flat roof repair take?

Small repairs may be completed quickly, while larger repairs depend on roof condition, access, weather, materials, and leak complexity.

Can you repair flat roofs in winter?

Some emergency repairs can be performed in colder weather, but permanent repairs depend on temperature, moisture, safety, and product requirements.

Do flat roof repairs need maintenance afterward?

Yes. Flat roofs should be inspected, cleaned, and maintained regularly to reduce future leaks and extend roof life.

How often should a flat roof be inspected?

Flat roofs should generally be inspected at least once or twice per year and after major storms, high winds, heavy rain, snow, or rooftop work.

What signs show my flat roof needs repair?

Warning signs include leaks, ceiling stains, ponding water, bubbles, cracks, open seams, loose flashing, soft spots, mold, and recurring interior moisture.
