Understanding Commercial Roofing Cost Per Square Foot

When planning a commercial roofing project, one of the first questions is: How much does commercial roofing cost per square foot? The answer depends on the material, roof size, complexity, and your location. Below we break down the typical price ranges for the most popular commercial roofing systems.

TPO Roofing Cost Per Square Foot

TPO (Thermoplastic Polyolefin) is a single‑ply membrane known for its energy efficiency and durability. The commercial roofing cost per square foot for TPO typically ranges from $4.50 to $8.00. This includes materials, labor, and basic accessories. TPO is ideal for flat roofs and offers excellent UV reflectivity, reducing cooling costs.

EPDM Roofing Cost Per Square Foot

EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) is a synthetic rubber membrane that has been used for decades. The commercial roofing cost per square foot for EPDM is generally $4.00 to $7.50. EPDM is highly flexible and resistant to extreme temperatures, making it a reliable choice for low‑slope roofs.

Metal Roofing Cost Per Square Foot

Metal roofing offers exceptional longevity and durability. For corrugated metal panels, the commercial roofing cost per square foot ranges from $5.50 to $10.00. For standing seam metal roofs, which provide a sleek, modern appearance and superior weather resistance, the cost is higher: $8.00 to $14.00 per square foot. Metal roofs can last 40–60 years with proper maintenance.

Standing Seam Metal Roofing Cost Per Square Foot

Standing seam metal roofing is a premium option for commercial buildings. The commercial roofing cost per square foot for standing seam typically falls between $8.00 and $14.00. This system features concealed fasteners and raised seams that provide excellent water shedding and a clean, professional look. It is ideal for high‑end commercial projects.

Factors That Affect Commercial Roofing Cost Per Square Foot

Several factors influence the final price:

  • Roof size and complexity: Larger roofs often have a lower cost per square foot, while complex shapes, multiple penetrations, and steep slopes increase labor costs.
  • Material quality: Thicker membranes and higher‑grade metals cost more but offer longer warranties.
  • Insulation and deck preparation: Adding or replacing insulation, repairing the deck, or installing a cover board adds to the total.
  • Location: Labor rates vary by region. Staten Island rates are competitive with the New York metro area.
  • Contractor experience: Certified, experienced contractors may charge more but provide better workmanship and warranties.
