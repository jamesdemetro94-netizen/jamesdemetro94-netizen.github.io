What is TPO Roofing?

Thermoplastic Polyolefin – The Modern Roofing Solution

◺️

Single-Ply Membrane

TPO is a single-ply roofing membrane made from a blend of polypropylene and ethylene-propylene rubber. It's heat-weldable, creating watertight seams stronger than the membrane itself.

🌎

Energy Efficient

The reflective white surface reduces cooling costs by reflecting sunlight away from your building. TPO can lower energy bills by up to 20% in summer months.

💧

Waterproof Protection

Heat-welded seams create a seamless, watertight barrier that protects against leaks, wind uplift, and severe weather. Ideal for flat and low-slope roofs.

📈

20–30 Year Lifespan

With proper installation and maintenance, TPO roofs last 20–30 years. Thicker membranes (60 or 80 mil) offer even longer durability and better puncture resistance.

Why TPO is Better

Compared to EPDM, PVC, and traditional roofing materials

1

Energy Efficiency

TPO's reflective surface reduces heat absorption, lowering cooling costs by up to 20%. EPDM absorbs heat, making TPO the better choice for energy savings.

2

Durability

TPO resists tears, punctures, and impact better than EPDM. It also withstands UV radiation, chemicals, and ozone without degrading.

3

Cost-Effective

TPO installation costs are lower than PVC, and its long lifespan means fewer repairs and replacements over time. You get more value for your investment.

4

Environmentally Friendly

TPO is recyclable and reduces the urban heat island effect. Its energy efficiency also lowers your carbon footprint.
